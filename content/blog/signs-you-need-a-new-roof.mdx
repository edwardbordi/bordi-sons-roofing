A roof rarely fails overnight. It tells you it's wearing out — if you know
what to look for. After decades on South Jersey roofs, here are the five signs
we tell our neighbors to watch for.

## 1. Your roof is past 20 years old

Most asphalt shingle roofs last 20–25 years. If yours is in that range, it's
worth a free inspection even if it looks fine from the ground.

## 2. Shingles are curling, cracking, or missing

Curling or cracked shingles can't shed water the way they should. A few missing
shingles after a storm may be a repair; widespread damage usually isn't.

## 3. Granules in the gutters

Those sand-like granules protect your shingles from UV. Finding piles of them in
your gutters means the shingles are breaking down.

## 4. Daylight or stains in the attic

If you can see light through the roof boards — or you spot water stains on the
underside — water is already getting in. Don't wait.

## 5. A sagging roofline

Sagging points to a structural or moisture problem in the decking. This one is
not a wait-and-see item; have it looked at promptly.
